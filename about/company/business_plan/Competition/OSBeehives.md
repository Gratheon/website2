## Overview

OSBeehives (Open Source Beehives) focuses on educational and urban beekeeping with open source hardware designs and monitoring software.

## Product Features
- Open source beehive designs
- Educational focus
- Sensor monitoring capabilities
- Web-based data visualization
- Community-driven development
- Urban beekeeping emphasis
- DIY-friendly approach

## Competitive Analysis

### Strengths
- Open source approach (similar to Gratheon)
- Educational market positioning
- Urban beekeeping focus (underserved niche)
- Community contributions
- Lower cost through DIY
- Transparency builds trust
- Good for learning/beginners

### Weaknesses vs Gratheon
- More focused on hardware designs than software
- Educational market (smaller TAM than commercial)
- Limited computer vision capabilities
- Basic sensor features
- Less sophisticated ML/AI
- Smaller community than Gratheon
- Urban focus limits scalability

### Strategic Implications
- Fellow open source project (collaboration opportunity)
- Educational market is separate from Gratheon's semi-pro target
- Urban beekeeping is interesting niche
- Open source validation in beekeeping
- Could partner on hardware designs
- Different market segments (complementary, not competitive)

## Market Position
- Niche player (education + urban)
- Open source community
- Hardware design focus

## Partnership Opportunities
- Share open source hardware designs
- Collaborate on educational content
- Cross-promote to respective communities
- Joint workshops or tutorials
- Contribute to each other's projects

## Lessons for Gratheon
- Educational market values open source
- Urban beekeeping is growing segment
- Hardware design sharing benefits community
- Balance DIY with plug-and-play options
- Open source attracts contributors

## Strategic Notes
- Not a direct competitor (different markets)
- Potential collaboration partner
- Validates open source approach
- Educational content could expand Gratheon reach
